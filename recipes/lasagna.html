2 Minute Lasagna Recipe

The Backstory You Never Asked For

Growing up in the small town of Nu Pastah, I remember italian cousine being an essential part of our lifestyle. We ate nothing else but various pasta dishes, so there was no choice but to get extremely creative with recipes. As a community, there is a common known practice called "Bulls Hitting", (I know, weird name, and I have no idea where it comes from!) for those days when creativity and innovation is lacking.

Bulls Hitting is the tradition of inviting your friends and family over for a lavish pasta dinner, and making it up as you go. Really. You write random food items on a sheet with whatever measurements you deem appropriate, and wing it! You either end up with a horrible dish and priceless memories from pranking your guests, or you make a great dish. With that being said, here is one dish my sister and I came up with through this great tradition. If I had one piece of advice for you going into this, it would be to trust the process. You will think this recipe is ridiculous, but I promise it will rock your socks off!


What you'll need:

  • 3 cups freezing water, separated
  • 3 Small baking sheets
  • Salt and pepper
  • Frezer

The prep time for this lasagna is 2 minutes, but freeze time will take 1 hour. Lets get started!

  1. Have all of your ingredients measured and ready.
  2. Grease your baking sheets HEAVILY.
  3. Pour 1 cup of water into each small baking sheet.
  4. Place all 3 small baking sheets in the freezer for 1 hour, or until solid.
  5. Once solid, remove from freezer.
  6. Lift your solid ice layers out of the baking sheets, and place them on top of one another.

Garnish your lasagna with salt and pepper to taste, if you wish! Enjoy!
